Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 25, 2025

1. Introduction

HealthFlow ("we," "our," or "the app") is a blood pressure and health tracking application designed to help users manage their health data, particularly for those monitoring IgA Nephropathy. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our platform.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information when you use HealthFlow.

2. Data Collection & Storage

HealthFlow collects the following personal health information:

  • Account Information: Email address, password
  • Profile Information: Full name, age, gender, height, weight
  • Health Data: Blood pressure readings (systolic, diastolic, pulse), date, and time
  • Calculated Data: BMI (Body Mass Index)

Where Your Data Is Stored:

By default, all data is stored locally on your device using the browser's sessionStorage API. This means your information never leaves your device and is automatically deleted when your browser tab or window is closed.

4. Data Retention

Local Storage (Default): All data stored in sessionStorage is automatically deleted when:

  • You close the browser tab or window
  • You close the entire browser application
  • You clear your browser's session storage manually
  • You restart your device

Backend Storage (Optional): If you choose to use our optional backend service (MongoDB + server), data persists until you explicitly delete your account. See section 5 for more details.

5. Backend Integration (Optional)

HealthFlow can optionally integrate with a backend database (MongoDB Atlas) for persistent, cross-device access. If enabled:

  • Data is encrypted during transmission (HTTPS)
  • Passwords are hashed using bcrypt before storage
  • Authentication uses secure JWT tokens
  • Data is protected with secure HTTP-only cookies

Your choice: You can use HealthFlow with temporary local storage (default) or opt-in to backend synchronization for permanent cross-device storage.

7. Security Recommendations for Production

Before deploying HealthFlow in a production healthcare environment, we recommend:

  1. Implement password hashing (bcryptjs or Argon2)
  2. Use HTTPS/TLS for all communications
  3. Deploy a secure backend with authentication (JWT or OAuth2)
  4. Use a managed database service (MongoDB Atlas) with encryption
  5. Implement input validation and sanitization
  6. Add audit logging for all data access
  7. Consider HIPAA compliance if used for clinical purposes
  8. Implement secure password recovery mechanisms
